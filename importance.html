1.Biodiversity Support: Ecosystems provide a natural habitat for a vast range of species, helping to maintain biodiversity. Diverse ecosystems support various plants, animals, and microorganisms, which contribute to resilient and stable environmental systems.
2.Climate Regulation: Ecosystems like forests, oceans, and wetlands play a crucial role in regulating the Earth's climate. They absorb carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases, helping to mitigate climate change by balancing atmospheric conditions.
3.Natural Resource Provision: Ecosystems supply essential resources such as clean water, timber, food, and medicinal plants. These resources are vital for human survival, economic activities, and the production of goods.
4.Soil Fertility and Agriculture: Healthy ecosystems promote soil fertility through nutrient cycling, which is essential for agriculture. Decomposers and other organisms contribute to soil health, supporting crop growth and sustainable food production.
